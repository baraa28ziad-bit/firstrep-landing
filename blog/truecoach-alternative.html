Best TrueCoach Alternative in 2026: A Detailed Feature Comparison

February 23, 2026 10 min read

TL;DR: FirstRep is the best TrueCoach alternative in 2026. It starts free for 3 clients (TrueCoach has no free tier), includes a built-in client marketplace, complete scheduling with Google Calendar + Meet, structured coaching with check-in queues and risk scoring, and burnout prevention features. Every feature is included on every plan — no gating, no surprises. FirstRep costs less than TrueCoach at every client count.

TrueCoach has been a solid choice for personal trainers who want clean, no-nonsense workout delivery. It does the basics well: you build workouts, assign them to clients, and they log their results. The interface is clean, the learning curve is gentle, and for trainers who just need a digital clipboard, it works.

But here is the thing — the fitness coaching industry has changed dramatically since TrueCoach launched. Clients expect more than a workout plan in their inbox. They want structured coaching, accountability systems, real-time communication, and progress intelligence. And trainers? They need a platform that does not just deliver workouts but actually helps them find clients, retain them, and build a real business.

If you have been feeling like TrueCoach is holding you back, you are not alone. Here is an honest, feature-by-feature comparison of TrueCoach versus FirstRep — so you can decide whether it is time to make the switch.

Where TrueCoach Falls Short (For Growing Trainers)

Let us be clear: TrueCoach is not a bad product. It is a focused one. But that focus comes with real limitations that become obvious as your business grows past a handful of clients.

No Built-In Client Marketplace

This is the single biggest gap. TrueCoach is purely a coaching delivery tool. It assumes you already have clients — or that you will find them yourself through Instagram, word of mouth, or paid ads. There is no discovery mechanism, no way for potential clients to browse trainers, filter by specialty, or read reviews within the platform.

For trainers who are already established with a full roster, this might be fine. But for the vast majority of independent trainers — the ones spending 10+ hours per week on marketing — this is a massive problem. You are paying for a tool that manages clients but does nothing to help you get them.

FirstRep includes a built-in marketplace where clients actively search for trainers by specialty, location, price, rating, and availability. Think of it as the difference between having a restaurant with no sign on the door versus being listed on Google Maps with photos, reviews, and directions.

Pricing Scales With Client Count

TrueCoach uses a tiered pricing model based on the number of active clients. The Starter plan at $30/month gets you up to 5 clients. The Standard plan jumps to $50/month for up to 20 clients. And if you need more than 20, you are looking at $80/month or more on the Pro plan. These costs add up fast, especially when you consider that TrueCoach does not help you earn that money back through client acquisition.

FirstRep takes a fundamentally different approach. The Free tier supports up to 3 clients with every feature included — no gating, no restrictions. The Pro plan is $19/month plus $1.50 per client above 8. That means a trainer with 25 clients pays $44.50/month instead of $80 — and gets significantly more features.

Limited Coaching Framework

TrueCoach offers basic messaging and the ability to add notes to workouts. But it does not have a structured coaching system. There is no formal weekly check-in flow, no activation tracking for new clients, no "needs attention" dashboard that surfaces at-risk clients, no automated monthly reports, and no client risk scoring.

If you want to deliver structured, repeatable coaching at scale, you end up building your own system using Google Forms, spreadsheets, and calendar reminders. TrueCoach gives you the tools to assign workouts. It does not give you the tools to coach.

No AI Program Builder

Building workout programs from scratch is one of the most time-consuming parts of being a trainer. TrueCoach offers templates and a solid exercise library, but there is no AI assistance. Every program is manual. Every variation is manual. Every periodization adjustment is manual.

FirstRep includes an AI program builder that can generate complete programs based on client goals, experience level, available equipment, and time constraints. You review, tweak, and assign — instead of building from a blank page every time. For trainers managing 15+ clients, this alone can save 5-10 hours per week.

No Integrated Scheduling or Booking

TrueCoach does not include any scheduling functionality. If you offer in-person sessions, virtual consultations, or assessment calls, you need a separate tool — Calendly, Acuity, or something similar. That means another monthly subscription, another login, another system that does not talk to your client data.

FirstRep has built-in scheduling with availability management, appointment types, calendar sync (including Google Calendar), and even automatic Google Meet links for virtual sessions. Everything lives in one place.

No Habit Tracking or Gamification

Modern coaching goes beyond sets and reps. Habits like sleep, hydration, step count, and meal prep compliance are often more important than the workout itself. TrueCoach has no habit tracking system, no streaks, no badges, no challenges, and no gamification mechanics.

FirstRep includes a complete habit tracking system with customizable habits, streak tracking, badges for milestones, and group challenges. These features dramatically improve client engagement and retention — the two metrics that actually determine whether your business grows.

Who Should Stay With TrueCoach?

We want to be fair. TrueCoach is a good product for certain use cases, and switching platforms always has a cost. Here is who should probably stay:

  • You have fewer than 5 clients and no plans to grow. If you are a part-time trainer with a small, stable roster, TrueCoach's simplicity might be all you need.
  • You only need workout delivery. If your coaching model is strictly "send workouts, get logs back" with no check-ins, no habit tracking, and no structured accountability, TrueCoach does that cleanly.
  • You do not need a marketplace. If all your clients come from referrals and you never struggle to fill your roster, the marketplace is not a differentiator for you.
  • You love TrueCoach's simplicity. TrueCoach is deliberately minimal. Some trainers prefer that. If you find feature-rich platforms overwhelming and just want a clean workout delivery tool, TrueCoach's stripped-down approach might genuinely be a better fit for your workflow.
  • You are deeply integrated with TrueCoach's ecosystem. If you have years of client data, custom templates, and established workflows in TrueCoach, the switching cost is real. Make sure the benefits justify the migration effort.

There is no shame in staying with a tool that works for you. The question is whether it will still work for you in 12 months as your business evolves.

How to Switch From TrueCoach to FirstRep

Migration is always the scary part. Here is a step-by-step guide to make the transition as smooth as possible.

  1. Export your client list from TrueCoach. Download your client contact information, notes, and any relevant training history. TrueCoach allows you to export client data from your account settings. Make sure you have email addresses for every active client.
  2. Create your FirstRep trainer profile. Sign up (it is free), complete your profile with your specialties, certifications, bio, and a promo video if you have one. This is also your marketplace listing, so make it count.
  3. Set up your packages and pricing. Use FirstRep's package builder to recreate your current offerings — monthly coaching, session packs, one-time programs, or whatever you sell. Add discount codes if you want to offer switching incentives to existing clients.
  4. Rebuild your programs with AI assistance. This is where you save the most time. Instead of manually recreating every program, use the AI builder to generate base programs and then customize them. What used to take hours takes minutes. Transfer your favorite custom templates too.
  5. Set up scheduling and availability. If you offer in-person or virtual sessions, configure your availability slots, appointment types, and connect your Google Calendar. Enable Google Meet auto-links for virtual sessions.
  6. Invite your existing clients. Use FirstRep's invite system to send personalized email invitations, shareable links, or QR codes. These clients come in as "external" clients with 0% marketplace commission — your revenue stays yours.
  7. Test before going live. Assign a test workout to yourself or a friend. Walk through the client experience. Make sure messaging works, check-ins are configured, and payments are connected. Spend 30 minutes as a "client" so you know exactly what they will see.
  8. Communicate the switch to clients. Send a clear message explaining the switch, emphasizing the benefits they will get (better app experience, check-ins, habit tracking, progress reports). Include a direct link to download the app and accept your invite.

Most trainers complete the full migration in a weekend. The AI program builder is the biggest time saver — rebuilding 10 custom programs that would take 5+ hours on TrueCoach takes about 45 minutes with AI assistance.

TrueCoach delivers workouts. FirstRep delivers a coaching business. Choose based on where you want to be in 12 months, not where you are today.
